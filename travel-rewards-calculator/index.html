How to Maximize Travel Rewards

Travel rewards can significantly reduce your travel costs, but only if you understand how to extract maximum value from your points and miles.

Know Your Point Values

Not all points are created equal. Airline miles redeemed for business class flights can be worth 3 cents each, while the same miles redeemed for cash might only be worth 0.5 cents. Understanding the per-point value for each redemption option helps you choose wisely.

Transfer for Maximum Value

Transferable points (Chase Ultimate Rewards, Amex Membership Rewards, Citi ThankYou) are often the most valuable because you can move them to the airline or hotel partner offering the best deal at any given time.

Book Award Flights Strategically

The best redemptions come from business and first class international flights, where the per-mile value can exceed 5 cents. Domestic economy flights tend to offer the lowest per-mile value.

Earning vs. Burning

Focus on earning through sign-up bonuses (often 50,000-100,000 points) and category bonuses (3-5x on travel and dining). Everyday spending at 1x is less important than strategic sign-up bonuses.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do airline miles expire?

Most US airline miles now have no expiration as long as you have any account activity within 24 months. Some international carriers still have expiration policies, so check your specific program.

Is it better to use points or pay cash?

Use points when the redemption value exceeds 1.5 cents per point. If you can book a flight for $200 or use 25,000 miles (0.8 cents each), paying cash is usually better. But if that same flight costs $500, the miles are worth 2 cents each — a great deal.

How do transfer bonuses work?

Credit card programs periodically offer transfer bonuses (e.g., 30% extra when transferring to a specific airline). This effectively increases your points balance by 30% for that transfer, making it an excellent time to book award travel.

What is the minimum points needed for a free flight?

Domestic economy saver awards typically start at 12,500-25,000 miles each way. International economy can be found for 30,000-60,000 miles round trip. Business class international awards generally require 100,000-200,000 miles.
