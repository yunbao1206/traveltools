Do You Need Travel Insurance?

Travel insurance protects your financial investment in a trip when unexpected events force you to cancel, interrupt, or seek medical care while traveling. For most trips, the cost of insurance is a small fraction of the total trip expense, but the protection it offers can be invaluable.

When Travel Insurance Makes Sense

If you have significant prepaid, non-refundable expenses (flights, hotels, tours), travel insurance is worth considering. It's especially important for international travel where your domestic health insurance may not cover medical emergencies. Trips involving adventure activities, cruises, or complex itineraries also benefit from coverage.

Types of Coverage

Basic plans typically cover trip cancellation, trip interruption, and emergency medical expenses. Comprehensive plans add baggage loss, travel delays, and higher medical limits. Cancel For Any Reason (CFAR) coverage provides the most flexibility, allowing you to cancel for reasons not covered by standard policies, though it costs more.

What Affects the Cost

Travel insurance premiums depend primarily on the total trip cost, your age, trip duration, destination, and coverage level. Older travelers typically pay more due to higher medical risk. Longer trips and more expensive destinations also increase premiums. The coverage type you select has the biggest impact on price, with CFAR plans costing roughly twice as much as basic coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does travel insurance usually cost?

Travel insurance typically costs between 4% and 12% of your total trip cost, depending on coverage level. Basic plans run about 4-5%, comprehensive plans 6-8%, and Cancel For Any Reason (CFAR) coverage 8-12%. Your age, trip length, and destination also affect the final price.

Is travel insurance worth it for domestic trips?

It depends on your situation. For domestic trips with flexible bookings and minimal prepaid expenses, you may not need it. However, if you have non-refundable flights, hotel bookings, or tour reservations, travel insurance can protect that investment. Domestic coverage is also typically less expensive than international coverage.

What does Cancel For Any Reason (CFAR) cover?

CFAR coverage allows you to cancel your trip for any reason at all, including reasons not covered by standard trip cancellation policies (like fear of traveling, work obligations, or simply changing your mind). It typically reimburses 50-75% of your prepaid costs and must be purchased within a certain window after your initial trip deposit.

Does my credit card provide travel insurance?

Many premium travel credit cards include some travel protections, such as trip cancellation insurance, baggage delay coverage, and rental car insurance. However, coverage limits and exclusions vary significantly. Check your card's benefit guide carefully and compare it against a standalone policy before relying on it as your only protection.
